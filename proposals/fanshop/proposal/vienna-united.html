Overview

What we're replacing and what we're building.

The current shop is a WordPress form with a Stripe widget at the bottom. Fans tick checkboxes and submit. Orders land in an inbox and get processed by hand.

We're building the replacement: a storefront for fans and an admin panel you manage yourself.

What We Found

We looked at the current shop.

viennaunited.eu/vienna-united-shop — here's what we saw:

viennaunited.eu/vienna-united-shop/
  • 01
    No cart — customers tick checkboxes to order Items are listed as checkboxes on a single page. No product photos, no sizes visible upfront. Someone wanting two items fills in one long form and submits.
  • 02
    Every order is processed manually Each form submission arrives as an email someone has to read and act on. There's no order queue.
  • 03
    No order history for customers No confirmation email goes out, no order number. Customers have no record of what they ordered.
  • 04
    No admin panel — product updates require editing WordPress Changing a price or adding a new product means editing the WordPress page directly. There's no dashboard. You need to know WordPress to update anything.
  • 05
    Stripe is bolted directly onto the form Payment is a Stripe iframe at the bottom of the form. No receipt goes out, no audit trail exists on the club's side. Just a payment widget stuck to a checkbox list.
The Solution

The platform.

We're building two things: a public storefront for fans and members, and an admin panel for whoever runs the shop.

The storefront works like any real shop: browse, add to cart, pay. The admin panel is where orders land — sorted by team since deliveries go to training, ready to mark off as they go out.

What fans & members see
  • Product catalog with real photos and descriptions
  • Size selection and personalization (name + number for jerseys)
  • Shopping cart — multiple items, one checkout
  • Cards, PayPal, EPS, and Klarna at checkout
  • Order confirmation email sent at checkout
  • Order history in their account
  • Member-only items or early access (optional)
What the admin sees
  • All orders in one view, sorted by team for training delivery
  • Add or remove products and change prices yourself — no developer needed
  • Mark orders as in production, ready, or delivered
  • Upload product photos from the dashboard

Payment methods chosen based on Austrian market data: cards cover 38% of online transactions, PayPal is used by 73% of Austrian shoppers, EPS is Austria's domestic bank transfer built into local bank accounts, and Klarna covers the 38% who prefer pay-later. All four run through Stripe.
