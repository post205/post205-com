# POST 205 — Core Values

## The foundation

**Culture is what we incentivize and make easy.**

This applies to both: how we build (what we make simple for ourselves and collaborators) and how we operate as a company (what we reward in client relationships, in work, in decisions).

If we say we value something but make it hard or ignore it when it happens, it isn't a value — it's a wish.

## Values (working list)

*Add to this as they become clear through decisions made, not aspirations stated.*

### Activation — give the win fast

The sooner you give a client or partner their first real win, the sooner they're activated. Activation is not a status — it's a moment. A client who gets value quickly stays. A supplier who gets a clear brief and fast feedback comes back.

This applies to how we onboard clients, how we brief suppliers, and how we build systems: make the first win obvious and achievable. Design the path to it.

**How we build this in:** The ops dashboard surfaces who hasn't been activated yet. It doesn't do the work — it makes it impossible to ignore.

### Activation and the 4Rs

Once activated, the relationship has a natural progression: Retain → Review → Refer → Resell. Each stage is a win — for the client, for POST 205, for both. The system makes the next step visible. Toffer decides when to move.

- **Retain:** Stay useful. Fast support. Check-ins that mean something.
- **Review:** Ask right after their win, when it's still fresh.
- **Refer:** Give them something they can share. Two-sided rewards work best.
- **Resell:** Timing matters. Reach out when signals are there — usage, limits, goals hit.

*Source: post205.com/frameworks/4rs — Toffer's published framework. 2026-04-28*

## How this applies to building

Every system we build for a client should reflect this: what does the system make easy? What does it reward with less friction? Those are the implicit values of that system.

When building ops, the website, or a client portal — ask: what behavior does this design incentivize?
